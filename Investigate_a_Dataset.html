Introduction¶

This dataset collects information from 100k medical appointments in Brazil and is focused on the question of whether or not patients show up for their appointment. A number of characteristics about the patient are included in each row. In fact, two main questions needed to be answred. The first question is about the percentage of people who showed up vs people who didn't showed up. The second question invetegating the features that can be used to predict if the patients will attend thier appointments or not.

Limitations¶

Every study has certain limitations, and this one is not an exception. In fact, there are missing festures that could be beneficial for this study such as the distance between the patient home and the hospital. In addition, some data is not accurate such as patient age, some rows contain zero and negative values.

Conclusion¶

• It can be clearly seen that younger patients tend to miss their scheduled appointments.

• It can be clearly seen that women are most likely miss thier scheduled appointments when compared to men

• We can see that patients with scholarships tend to miss thier scheduled appointments compared to patients who do not have scholarships

• It seems that alcoholism does not affect the appointments attendance

• It seems that patients with hypertension tend to attend thier appointments

• It seems that patients with Diabetes tend to attend thier appointments

• It can be cleatly sean that handicapped patients in general tend to miss their scheduled appointments

• It can be clear that sendeing a rminder message does not impove the attendancy of the patients
